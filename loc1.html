Call for Applications: Art Project “Uare Qalaqs”

Contest Rules

Eligibility

The project is open to all interested artists. We are pleased to invite all professional and amateur artists, architects, sculptors, designers, painters, photographers, video artists and persons working in other media to participate in the contest. One project can be submitted by an artist or group of artists, though the maximum amount of money designated for each project and honorarium will not change in this case. The number of applications submitted by each applicant is not limited. Artists should be able to complete the work and cooperate with the organization’s team in the given terms. By participating in the contest, applicants confirm, that they have read and accepted the project rules.

The winner must be present in Tbilisi from March 9 - May 31, 2015, for the interview and installation development works. In case of group applications, a point person should be appointed as responsible for the project. He/she will oversee the installation construction and placement process.

Ownership

The project participants will retain all rights to the idea and design. “Iare Pekhit” retains the right to create printed and digital material with regard to the artist’s work. Original application and accompanying materials (description, budget, and sketches) will be kept in the organization’s archive.

Installation

Those willing to participate must create original, functionally integrated installations, that will be exhibited in one of the five selected public spaces of Tbilisi for a period of one month. The applicants shall ensure the durability of the installation, which must be capable of withstanding all weather conditions for the duration of the display. No changes may be made to any existing trees, plantings, or landscape elements. The installation must be respectful to pedestrians, drivers and vehicles. It shall not impose a threat to the existing environment or create obstacles. Preference will be given to bold, provocative and inspirational projects.

Project Locations

We collaborate with the relevant departments of the Tbilisi City Hall and assist the artists in the technical aspects of project placement. You may find detailed information, map coordinates, plans and photo material of each project territory by clicking on the links below. The proposed installation, must be produced within the red borders drawn around the project plan. It is optional, whether you choose to cover the whole marked territory for your installation. Five project areas were selected for the contest:

  • The Right Bank, The Slope of Nikoloz Baratashvili Bridge
  • Hero Square, Area In front of the Circus Building Staircase
  • The Left Bank, The Slope of Vakhushti Bridge (on the side of Didube)
  • Rustaveli, Rustaveli Ave./Elbakidze Slope Corner
  • Saburtalo, Pekini Ave./Vazha Pshavela Ave. Junction

Jury

A competent jury will decide on the winning projects. The contest will be held in two stages: the first stage considers review and short-listing of the best applications. 5 winners will be determined at the second stage of the contest. The jury will give preference to submissions, that feature realistic and concrete plans for accomplishing the work within the given schedule and budget. The jury also reserves the right to not choose a winner if, an insufficient number of eligible and/or qualified entries are received.

Budget

The maximum budget allocated for each art project is 1,500 GEL. This budget includes all related costs for project production: procurement of all materials, transportation and labor costs, equipment rentals, installation elements, placement and removal, as well as printing costs. Detailed description and explanation of all the abovementioned must be articulated in the application. Submission of the complete project budget is obligatory for all applicants. “Iare Pekhit” will supervise the use of the budget throughout the process.

Award

5 winning projects will be determined through the open contest. The authors of the winning projects will receive requested funding (up to 1,500 GEL) for the production and placement of the installations. After the submission of the corresponding financial report and completion of the work, each winning artist (group) will be awarded a 1,000 GEL honorarium.

Submission

“Iare Pekhit” manages the electronic application process. Submissions must include a completed application form, with the written description of the project, a detailed budget as well as visual materials that clearly demonstrate the project concept and the artist’s approach. All applicants must become familiar with the given project rules and regulations, and submit a completed application form, along with the requested attachment files.

Iare Pekhit wants YOU to take part in the public art project “Uare Qalaqs” (Care for the City). Are you going to be 1 of the 5 winning artists to present your street installation in one of the five chosen public areas of Tbilisi?

For our project, we are looking for artists, who can transform the given spaces and play with the way the inhabitants see and feel the city. Artists, who are able to drag the spectator out of their comfort zones and have them collaborate, initiate and develop new things together.

We also love to walk and we want to encourage pedestrianism, show its role in our lives in an artistic, sharp, and preferably, poetic manner!

In a city, where the needs of humans are not clearly articulated, voiced and defended, and officials do not have the tools to effectively listen and account, public space concerns are not addressed. The challenge is huge, but “Uare Qalaqs” is an engagement experiment, to open up channels of communication between the community, professionals and officials through public art!

Public events, gatherings, protests or other activity will take place at each project opening around the theme proposed by the artist! It could be you!

The project is implemented with the support of the UN Democracy Fund and The Tbilisi City Hall.
